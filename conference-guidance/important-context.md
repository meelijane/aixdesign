A couple of things we've learned from running a lot of these:

A few key lessons we've learned from running conferences:

Rather than opening with an introduction to yourself and your company, leave that for the end. Our MC will introduce you to the audience before you take the stage — they'll know who you are and where you work. Use your opening seconds to hook them with your idea instead. End with a slide that has your name, role, social handles, and a QR code linking to your resources, portfolio, or slides. People will photograph that final slide — it's by far the most effective way to drive follow-up engagement.

A note on demos. If you're demoing software, pre-record it as video where you can — live demos rarely survive contact with conference networks and back-row sightlines.

Think high-cadence, high-signal. Trust that your audience has read the blog posts and seen the talks — give them what those don't say. The "what I actually learned" and "what I'd do differently."
