## Garbage in, garbage out __section header__
**Image:** pixel art of a garbage can, arrow pointing to computer, arrow pointing to another garbage can with a sparkle emoji 

**Speaker notes:**
- If you take one thing away, it’s this — AI is only as good as the context it has.

### Three ingredients to avoid slop

- Organisational context is key
- Personal context is also critical
- Specificity and iteration

**Speaker notes:**
- These three ingredients are what I want to talk about in this section.
- The quality of AI output depends on both your organisational context and personal context.
- And it depends on how specific you are and how you iterate.

### Organisational context is key

- Teamwork Graph provides unified context
- But it can’t capture *why* or *how*

**Speaker notes:**
- Atlassian has built a foundational layer called Teamwork Graph.
- It connects everything in your cloud — your Jira tickets, Confluence pages, users, teams, all the things.
- But it doesn’t capture *why* something happened, or the nuance of how decisions get made.

### Personal context is also critical

- Personal context = the missing layer
- How do we model intent and nuance?

**Speaker notes:**
- Even if we had perfect organisational data, we still miss personal context.
- What you were thinking, what you meant, the nuance of intent.
- That’s the hardest part to model.

### Specificity and iteration

- Specificity leads to better output
- Iteration improves quality

**Speaker notes:**
- You need to be extremely specific when working with AI.
- And you need to iterate like you would with a human collaborator.

## Quality is a team sport __section header__
**Image:** hands with pieces of the puzzle, pixel art style

**Speaker notes:**
- AI doesn’t just change how one person works — it changes how whole teams work.

### Don’t panic; organise

- The industry is moving fast
- Customers are cautious

**Speaker notes:**
- Everyone is racing, but you still need to be thoughtful.
- Enterprise customers are cautious, and trust is fragile.

### The industry is moving at lightspeed

- Atlassian is moving fast too
- But speed isn’t the only metric

**Speaker notes:**
- We’ve moved fast, but we’ve also learned to slow down when needed.

### Enterprise customers need to feel safe

- The burden is on us to build trust

**Speaker notes:**
- If we erode trust, customers will walk away.

### Eat your own dogfood, see how it tastes

- Dogfooding is in our DNA
- But it’s not enough

**Speaker notes:**
- We are culturally hard-wired to use our own tools — this is good and bad.
- Spending more time with customers is critically important & helps you identify the gaps.
- You don't want customers doing your QA.

## Strategic alignment matters __section header__
**Image:** animation of a bunch of arrows pointing in different directions, that then point towards the same point pixel art style

**Speaker notes:**
- If everyone runs in a different direction, you get duplicated effort and convergent thinking.

###

- We noticed a lot of convergent thinking
- Some experiences are harder to do agentically

**Speaker notes:**
- We saw teams independently building the same AI experiences.
- Some experiences just aren’t good fits for agents yet.

### We noticed a lot of convergent thinking

- Teams built similar AI features in parallel
- It was wasteful

**Speaker notes:**
- Example: multiple teams building chat-based patterns for totally different things.
- We had to course-correct.

### Some experiences are harder to do agentically

- Not everything should be a chat

**Speaker notes:**
- Premature convergence was a real issue — forcing chat everywhere.

### Don’t make easy things easier; make hard things easier

- Customers get annoyed when you get in their way with AI

**Speaker notes:**
- Hard problems are the unlock.
- Atlassian has many great opportunities for AI — natural language automations, format transfer, large data sets.
- The most value we can provide to customers is making their hardest tasks easier.
- Our researcher built a three-agent system to do literature review.

## Right tool for the job __section header__
**Image:** pixel art of a flat lay of a set of tools

**Speaker notes:**
- Work is diverse. The tools should be too.

### Work is diverse; your tools should be too

- Design process includes many tools
- AI is just one

**Speaker notes:**
- Match the tool to the job. AI is one tool among many.
- One great way to increase quality is to make it a clear goal every craft is working towards.
- But as a 15,000 person company, teams will still diverge and duplicate work all the time.

### AI to prototype vs AI to polish

- Different tools for different stages

**Speaker notes:**
- Stop arguing about which AI tool is best.
- Different tools are suitable at different stages of the process.
- Generative tools for prototyping, automated tools for polishing.

### Sometimes you need a pen

- Make it tangible

**Speaker notes:**
- Physical tools are still valuable. Sometimes you need a pen to think.

### Knowing when to leverage AI is a skill

- Discernment matters

**Speaker notes:**
- As a technical person (I was a software eng before I was a designer) I think I have underestimated this bit.
